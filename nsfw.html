Consent

This is perhaps my biggest kink. I love seeing people being willing and happy in all situations. They might even be facing trouble, and they'll gleefully and eagerly jump into it.

My stories practically always involve everyone being willing and consenting, often enthusiastically. If there's this incredible eagerness in action, it will be labeled "enthusiastic consent".

This is such a big kink that a clear sign of distress is often a major turn-off for me. My stories are happy; my roleplays are happy. Even when reading kinky smut, I much prefer willing participants; if they are unwilling, I usually just pick out the idea and daydream my own fantasies with it.

"Bad" end

A "bad end" means someone involved has reached a "game over", a situation they are unlikely to escape from. But "bad" is in quotation marks. In my scenarios, everyone always gives full consent, so even though it might be a bad end, people are still enjoying it.

Either way, a bad end often implies permanence; the subject is unlikely to be able to escape, if at all possible. That said, if they are loving it there, do they even want to leave?

Flattening

A character is flattened, reduced into two dimensions. Obviously requires magic and heavy suspension of disbelief to not cause bloody gore.

Being flattened generally also makes them unable to move, so there's an immobilization aspect to it.

Sky has this as a main kink, being a toon himself.

Immobilization

A character is restrained one way or another, in such a full and thorough situation that they are completely, absolutely unable to move. Often this involves completely encasing them in something, although other forms of immobilization can work. There's likely magic involved to keep the subject alive and aware, unless I go with the snuff route.

Some of my usual go-to ideas are: encasing someone in a block of clear resin; entombing someone in a sarcophagus or coffin that's filled with hardening liquid rubber; and flattening someone into a two-dimensional immobile picture and laminating them to preserve them that way.

Vee has this as a main kink, and Sky does naturally gravitate toward this too. It is one of my biggest kinks.

There's not much that's more powerful than rewriting reality itself. This can happen in many ways; these are just some that I've used.

Particularly powerful rewriting might even propagate this effect throughout the entire timeline, as if the fur/object had always been that way.

In short, it's kind of a catch-all tag for when something is obviously highly fantastical.

Explicit

A kink, by default, doesn't necessarily imply any sexually explicit stuff. It's possible to have, for example, a story involving flattening and immobilization that doesn't otherwise involve explicit stuff. (I did write one: Sealed.)

As such, this tag is for when there's actually such explicit stuff. Some people indulge in a lot of kinks but shy away from explicit stuff, so this is a way to warn them.

As an aside, for some reason, some furries think not being explicit automatically makes them SFW. I don't think so. If it's still meant to arouse or be pleasurable, then it's not SFW. On a scale of "general, mature, adult", such thing might be rated "mature" just fine, but it's definitely not "general".

The Civilization: Stillen

For more information about my alternate universe, The Civilization, check my furry page.

Stillen is a thema of the topa Hedonia. Hedonia is a world of pleasure, and it has several regions — themas — each focused on a specific kink. Stillen focuses on encasement and immobilization, with a good heaping of appreciation of art.

In Stillen, everyone are artists, using a special material for their artwork: fur bodies, whether of themselves or of other people. Statues, paintings, wall mosaics, keychains, and many other things are made of furs. People also have appreciation toward them, and people know they will be appreciated when they become art, which perhaps add a little bit of exhibitionism.

To make sure that the population doesn't instantly deplete to zero, Stillen has a massive system of cloning and regeneration. Any fur that has been "arted" — turned into inanimate art — will be cloned shortly after, with all the memories preserved; this way, they can share their experience in great detail to other people.

In the explanation about The Civilization, I mentioned that I had a writing project. It only had to take place here in Stillen; the rest of the universe was built to explain why Stillen could exist.
