🔝 🔗 Tags

Sure, this whole page might be "NSFW", but there are a whole lot of different kinds of NSFW. Many people are okay with some of them while not with others. That's why I'm giving tags (in teal) to individual links and works, so you can decide whether you want to be exposed to that stuff. You can think of tags as content warnings; alternatively, they can be keywords that you search for. Whatever purpose that strikes you the best.

Some tags are used a lot, so I'll just clarify the meaning here. (The reason a tag is used a lot is most likely because it's a big kink of mine.) Tags that are not clarified here generally have some sort of common sense meaning.

"Bad" end
People involved usually meet some sort of "bad" end. Generally this means whatever predicament they end up in lasts for a very long time, possibly forever. The quotation marks are because, should you consider it "bad" if they actively enjoy it? (See also: enthusiastic consent.) Also, if you're the kind of person that analyzes continuity so much, you might think, how does a character get a "bad" end in a story and appear well in another? Just treat the stories as non-canon, or the character gets cloned somehow, sheesh. It's just for fun.
Enthusiastic consent
Not only everyone involved willingly gets into the arrangement, but happily so. No sadness, no uncertainty, no regret. I've learned that my enjoyment of something is generally proportional to how enthusiastic everyone is. (Guess that's one reason I hate horror.)
Explicit
This means sexually explicit stuff. But wait, isn't NSFW equivalent to that? Not exactly. For some reason, some people in the furry fandom think that fetishy content that otherwise doesn't show genitals or anything "naughty" can be called safe/SFW. I think that's dumb; you very much don't want to be seen with that kind of stuff at work. One fur with nothing explicit showing that's eating another fur also with nothing explicit showing is still a fetishy vore piece (and so is NSFW), even if it's not explicit. In Fur Affinity, this would be the difference between "questionable" and "explicit" rating.
Flattening
This tag is exactly what it says. A character getting flattened, reduced to two dimensions. Doesn't it kill the character? Well, these stories are high fantasy already. No, they are alive, it's magic, next question. One of my characters, Sky, even has this property built-in; being a literal drawing coming to life, he can take being flattened back into a drawing no problem. Almost always, a flattened person can't move in their two dimensions.
Immobilization
A character is restrained one way or another. But that's just bondage. To deserve this tag, the restraints much be so thorough that there's basically zero movement possible, barely a twitch. I'm talking about things like being entombed in a coffin that's filled with hardening cement. That kind of zero movement.
Reality manipulation
One niche fetish I identified: when a character just bends the laws of reality and makes things happen just because they want to. Originally I put something called "eraserplay" here, where a character's body parts are erased from existence. I realized it's a special case of reality manipulation, since the latter can also add things, and it doesn't have to be body parts.

🔝 🔗 Stillen

Stillen is my attempt at worldbuilding, with the following core idea. I'm a huge fan of a pretty specific kink: immobilization with enthusiastic consent. (And, given that people happily get into the various states of immobilization, more often than not it will be permanent, so a "bad" end of sorts.) What kind of world would support this concept?

What I came up with is a galactic civilization that is technologically advanced, and at the same time, is peaceful and well off, allowing its citizens to explore whatever they want to their heart's content. The civilization spans numerous worlds, each focusing on a specific thing. Many of them are pretty routine: worlds for science, for art, for food, and there's even a world for Pokémon. But some worlds are focused in hedonism and pleasure, with many of them specializing in a certain kink. Stillen is one of them, focusing on immobilization.

It's fascinating to figure out, how does Stillen work? For example, there has to be a way to regenerate the people, otherwise its population would easily fall off. How does this regeneration work? Also, how is the world organized? It's an exercise in worldbuilding, letting me come up with creative thoughts; with the theming of my favorite fetish, I also end up enjoying thinking about it.

Of course, as it's worldbuilding, it can also serve as a setting for stories, and I intend to write future stories to take place in Stillen and the other worlds. I've also made it an open setting that others may interpret as they wish.
