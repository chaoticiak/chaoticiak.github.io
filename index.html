About me

My full name is Ivan Adrian Koswara, although I sometimes drop the middle name when I want to use a shorter name. I'm from Indonesia and was born in the 1990's. I study theoretical computer science, and (as of this writing) I am a Ph.D. student in National University of Singapore. I have a few publications.

Online, my username is chaotic_iak, sometimes with the underscore dropped. There's a story about this username (see below). Generally, I use my real name when interacting with people I first knew in real life, and my username when interacting with people I first knew online. I'm not a stickler with usernames; you can also call me "chao" (most common), "chaotic", or "iak". If you want to pronounce "chao", you can use either a "hard ch" sound (like chaos) or a "soft ch" sound (like choose).

Click for a story about my username

Back in my kid days, I was a big fan of the Sonic the Hedgehog franchise. (Now I'm much more a big fan of Pokémon instead, although I do still indulge in Sonic games from time to time.) Two of my favorite characters were Tails and Cream, and I knew there was something called "chao" in there. So I took them together as "Chao" + "T" for Tails + "C" for Cream, with an "i" to make it a full word, capitalized as "ChaoTiC". I added an identifier "iak" from my real name, and that was my username: ChaoTiC_iak.

Over time, I found this reasoning (and the capitalization) to be pretty dumb. I kept the letters, but dropped the capitalization, thereby also dropping the reference to Sonic.

I am also a furry. My main fursona is Sky, an anthro Shaymin Sky Forme. As such, I'm also often called Sky in furry circles. I love fluffy and cute things, and my favorite artists are representative of this.

In addition to the academic, I also like to solve and write puzzles. Logic puzzles, like Sudoku, are my favorite kind of puzzles; I've written a lot of logic puzzles. That said, I've also written other kinds of puzzles.

I'm also a gamer, although the games I like are generally niche. My favorite genres are puzzle games and rhythm games, although I also play other games (most notably the Pokémon series). I don't like realistic graphics and shy away from many AAA games. I value gameplay and innovation a lot, so I generally gravitate toward indie games that can go wild with their premise.
